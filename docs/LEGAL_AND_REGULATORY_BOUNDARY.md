# Legal and Regulatory Boundary

_Last updated: 2026-06-27_

## Operating posture

This repository is a public proof, evidence, architecture, and software-assurance surface around AGIJobManager. The default public site is read-only and does not request wallet access, token approvals, network switches, or Mainnet broadcasts. Expert operations are separated into an explicit Expert Console.

## Not a regulated service

The project is not positioned as a broker, dealer, exchange, marketplace operator, investment adviser, portfolio manager, money transmitter, custodian, bank, trust company, payment processor, law firm, accounting firm, auditor, cybersecurity certifier, insurance provider, employment agency, procurement adviser, or regulated professional service provider.

## Token and investment boundary

AGIALPHA is referenced only as a protocol utility and canonical identity. No page may state or imply expected profit, yield, appreciation, dividends, revenue share, ownership, equity, debt, management efforts for tokenholder profit, resale opportunity, investment strategy, or investment suitability.

## Privacy and GDPR posture

The implementation is designed for data minimization: no forms, no analytics, no cookies, no accounts, no customer database, and no intentional personal-data collection. Users must not submit personal data. If a user chooses to put data into GitHub, a wallet, a blockchain transaction, a URI, or an external service, the user is responsible for that data and the third-party provider's terms and law.

## Third-party boundary

GitHub, wallet providers, RPC providers, Etherscan, IPFS gateways, browsers, operating systems, cloud providers, and Ethereum Mainnet are independent third parties. The user selects and uses them independently. Their data practices, outages, terms, fees, mistakes, and legal obligations are not controlled by this repository.

## No universal immunity

No document can guarantee immunity from all laws, claims, regulators, or factual scenarios. The legal shield here is implemented as an engineering and publication posture: data avoidance, claim boundaries, explicit user responsibility, no investment language, no professional advice, no custody, and separated human-signed operations.
